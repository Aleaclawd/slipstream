# 2026 Market Trends — Grounding Brief

> Research compiled 2026-06-16 to ground **Slipstream** (selected via the GStack/Hermes
> Weekly Opportunity Prototype Intake, 2026-06-15: *"GTM Call-to-Action Copilot,"* niche =
> sales engineers / technical GTM, 119 pain signals over 8 days). Every product and
> business decision below traces back to one of these signals.

## 1. The market is large and compounding
- **Sales Tech market:** ~$49.5B (2025) → **$57.6B (2026)** → $192.7B (2034), **16.3% CAGR**.
- **Agentic AI market:** $7.6B (2025) → **~$10.8B (2026)**; broader AI spend growing **31.9%/yr** through 2029.
- **Gartner:** by end of 2026, **40% of enterprise apps embed task-specific AI agents** — up from <5% in 2025.
- Only **40% of B2B GTM teams** use deal-intelligence software today → large unpenetrated base.

**Implication:** We are early in a fast lane, not late to a saturated one. The category is forming *now*.

## 2. The defining shift of 2026: copilot → agent, intelligence → execution
- The buyer question changed from *"does it have AI?"* to **"can the AI complete the workflow end-to-end without my team managing each step?"**
- The revtech market is moving **"from revenue intelligence to revenue execution"** — practical action over dashboards.
- 2026's new standard is **"proactive signal synthesis"**: insights that *speak up* when something's off, rather than reports you have to go read.

**Implication:** Slipstream must ship **completed work** (an email you can send, a checklist you can run, CRM fields you can save, RFP rows you can paste) — not another dashboard.

## 3. Per-seat pricing is breaking — usage-based is the new default
- AI agents amplify individual output **5–10x**, so charging per seat *penalizes adoption of your own AI*.
- **85% of SaaS companies** have adopted usage-based pricing elements; pure per-seat is declining fast for AI-heavy products.
- Winning 2026 GTM = **hybrid PLG + SLG**: bottom-up self-serve acquisition (HubSpot/DocuSign/Atlassian model) + an enterprise sales layer for depth.

**Implication:** Lead with a **free, bottom-up wedge** (paste a transcript, get value in 60s) and price the paid tiers on **value/usage** (calls processed, seats, grounding library), not pure seats.

## 4. The niche is real and underserved: Sales Engineers / Solutions Consultants
- SEs **"spend too much time finding answers that already exist somewhere in Slack, Confluence, or a call recording from three months ago."**
- A **200-question RFP = 20+ hours** of pulling technical specs; **×4–5 concurrent opportunities** it consumes the time that should go to demos, POCs, and customer conversations.
- SEs are the **"most valuable and most reluctant contributors to the RFP process."**
- Best-in-class point tools hit **90% auto-fill** and **8x faster turnaround** — proof the workflow is automatable and buyers will pay for time back.

**Implication:** The SE's *post-call action workflow* (technical follow-up, demo/POC prep, security questionnaire & RFP, CRM technical-context sync) is a high-pain, high-value, under-owned wedge.

## 5. The hard constraint: grounding beats fluency
- **"AI-generated responses that hallucinate product features or misstate technical capabilities are worse than no response at all."** The tool must **ground outputs in verified content with clear source attribution.**

**Implication:** Source-attribution is a **first-class feature from MVP day one** — every extracted claim cites its evidence (transcript span / doc). This is our moat against generic summarizers and our answer to the #1 SE objection.

## 6. Competitive landscape (where we fit)
| Player | Focus | Price | Persona |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gong** | Conversation intelligence | ~$1,200–1,500/seat/yr | AE / managers |
| **Clari** | Pipeline / forecasting | ~$1,200–1,500/seat/yr | RevOps / leaders |
| **Sybill / Avoma / Grain** | Affordable CI + CRM sync | from ~$19–79/seat/mo | AE / SMB sellers |
| **SiftHub / Inventive / AutoRFP / 1up / Anchor** | RFP & knowledge auto-fill | varies | SE (RFP slice only) |

**The gap we own:** CI tools analyze the call *for managers*. RFP tools auto-fill *one* questionnaire. **Nobody owns the Sales Engineer's end-to-end "call → grounded next actions" loop.** That is Slipstream.
